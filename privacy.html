Privacy Policy

Effective April 26, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Anna H. Lee CPA ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, and protects information when you use our internal payroll application (the "Service"). The Service is provided exclusively to authorized employees and contractors of Anna H. Lee CPA for payroll administration. We've written this in plain language so it's easy to read.

1. Who we are

Anna H. Lee CPA is a California-based accounting practice. The Service is operated on behalf of the firm. If you have any questions about this policy or about the personal information we hold about you, you can contact us at chrischan9231@gmail.com.

2. What information we collect

We only collect the information needed to run payroll and meet our employer record-keeping obligations:

  • Identifiers — full name, email address, and an internal employee ID assigned by the administrator.
  • Contact and mailing address — street, city, state, and ZIP, used for W-2 forms and other tax documents.
  • Employment and compensation information — hours worked, pay rates, mileage and reimbursements, gross pay, taxes withheld, net pay, and pay-period dates.
  • Account and authentication data — sign-in records and session tokens generated when you click your magic-link to sign in.
  • Activity log entries — when paystubs are edited, voided, or finalized, we record who made the change and when, so the firm has an audit trail.

The Service does not currently collect or store Social Security numbers, bank account numbers, government IDs, biometric data, geolocation, browsing history, or marketing information.

3. How we use your information

We use the information we collect only for these purposes:

  • To calculate and produce paystubs, W-2 forms, and tax filings.
  • To meet legal employer record-keeping requirements (the IRS requires payroll records to be retained for at least 4 years).
  • To let employees view their own paystubs and year-end totals.
  • To maintain an audit trail of who made which changes to payroll data.

We do not sell your personal information, share it with advertisers, use it for marketing, or use it to train any AI or machine-learning model.

4. Where your data is stored

The Service stores your data in the United States using two infrastructure providers:

  • Supabase — our database and authentication platform. Data is encrypted in transit (HTTPS) and at rest. Access is controlled by row-level security so each employee can see only their own paystubs.
  • GitHub Pages — hosts the static front-end (HTML/CSS/JavaScript). No personal data is stored on GitHub Pages.

These providers act as data processors on our behalf and are contractually required to protect your information.

5. Who we share your data with

We share your information only when necessary:

  • Supabase, as described above, to store and serve your data.
  • Tax authorities (such as the IRS, the Franchise Tax Board, and the California Employment Development Department) when required by law for tax filings.
  • Other employees only see their own data. Only administrators can view all employees' data.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to anyone.

6. How long we keep your data

We retain payroll records for as long as required by federal and California law, which is generally at least four years after the tax year to which the records relate. After that retention period, we may delete or archive your data unless we have another legal obligation to keep it.

7. Your California privacy rights (CCPA / CPRA)

Because we operate in California and most or all of our employees are California residents, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), gives you specific rights regarding your personal information.

Right to Know

You can ask us what personal information we hold about you, where it came from, why we collected it, and who we've shared it with. We'll respond within 45 days.

Right to Delete

You can ask us to delete personal information we hold about you. Note that we may need to keep some information to comply with our legal obligations (for example, the IRS requires us to retain payroll records for at least four years), but we'll delete anything that isn't required and tell you what we kept and why.

Right to Correct

If any information we hold about you is wrong, you can ask us to correct it. For most fields (name, address, email), an administrator can update it directly in the Service.

Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information

The Service does not currently collect any category of "sensitive personal information" as defined by the CPRA, so there is nothing to limit. If that ever changes, we'll update this policy and tell you about your options.

Right to Opt Out of Sale or Sharing

We do not sell or share your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising, so there is nothing to opt out of. We don't intend to start.

Right to Non-Discrimination

We will not retaliate against you, deny you employment, or treat you differently because you exercised any of your privacy rights.

8. How to exercise your rights

To make any of the requests above, email chrischan9231@gmail.com from the email address on file with the firm. We may ask you a follow-up question or two to verify your identity before we act on the request, and we'll respond within 45 days.

9. Security

We protect your data with the standard tools the Service is built on: HTTPS for all traffic, encrypted database storage, row-level security so employees can only see their own data, and magic-link authentication so there are no passwords to leak. No system is perfectly secure, but we take reasonable steps to protect your information.

10. Children's privacy

The Service is intended for adult employees only and is not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 16.

11. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes to this policy, we'll update the "Effective" date at the top and let active users know the next time they sign in. Continued use of the Service after a change means you accept the updated policy.

12. Contact us

Questions or concerns? Email chrischan9231@gmail.com. We read every message.
